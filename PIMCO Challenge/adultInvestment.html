Investing 101:

Investing refers to the act of allocating resources, usually money, with the expectation of generating an income or profit. It is a way of using money to make more money, rather than keeping it in a savings account where it earns minimal interest. There are many different types of investments, each with their own level of risk and potential return.

Here is a list of some common types of investments:

  1. Stocks: Investing in stocks means buying shares of ownership in a publicly traded company. Stocks can provide a high return on investment (ROI), but they also come with a high level of volatility. The value of a stock can fluctuate greatly in a short period of time, making it a risky investment.

  2. Bonds: Bonds are a type of debt security in which an investor loans money to an entity, such as a corporation or government, in exchange for periodic interest payments. Bonds are generally considered to be less risky than stocks, but they also have a lower potential return.

  3. Real estate: Investing in real estate involves purchasing property with the expectation of generating income through rent or appreciation in value. Real estate investments can generate a steady stream of passive income, but they also require a significant amount of money up front and can be affected by market fluctuations.

  4. Mutual funds: Mutual funds are a type of investment that pools money from many investors to buy a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, or other securities. Mutual funds are generally considered to be less risky than individual stocks, but they also have a lower potential return.

  5. Certificates of Deposit: CDs are time deposits offered by banks, usually for a term of several months to a few years, with a fixed interest rate. They are considered to be one of the safest investments and have a low-risk and low-return.


Passive income refers to income that is generated from investments or other activities that require minimal ongoing effort. Passive income can come from investments such as rental properties, dividend-paying stocks, and peer-to-peer lending platforms. Passive income can provide a steady stream of income without the need for constant effort and can be a good way to diversify your investment portfolio.

It is important to note that the ROI and volatility of an investment can vary greatly depending on many factors, such as the type of investment, the market conditions, and your own risk tolerance. It's always a good idea to do your own research and consult a financial advisor before making any investment decisions.
