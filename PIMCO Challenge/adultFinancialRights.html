Adults

Financial Rights:

Adults should be aware of their financial rights in order to protect themselves and their assets. Here are some important financial rights to be aware of:

  1. Right to Privacy: Adults have the right to privacy in their financial dealings, including the right to keep their financial information confidential.

  2. Right to Fair Credit Reporting: Adults have the right to access their credit report and to dispute any inaccuracies. They also have the right to know who has accessed their credit report.

  3. Right to Fair Lending: Adults have the right to fair and equal treatment in obtaining credit, regardless of their race, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age, or any other legally protected characteristic.

  4. Right to Fair Debt Collection: Adults have the right to be free from abusive, deceptive, and unfair debt collection practices. They also have the right to dispute a debt and request validation of the debt.

  5. Right to Choose a Bank: Adults have the right to choose a bank that best meets their financial needs and to switch banks if they so choose.

  6. Right to File a Complaint: Adults have the right to file a complaint with the appropriate regulatory agencies if they believe their financial rights have been violated.

  7. Right to Financial Education: Adults have the right to access financial education and resources to help them make informed decisions about their finances.

  8. Right to Sue: Adults have the right to sue if they believe their financial rights have been violated, including the right to sue for damages or to seek an injunction.


By being aware of their financial rights, adults can take steps to protect themselves and their assets and to seek recourse if their rights have been violated. It is important to stay informed and to seek the advice of a financial professional if necessary.
